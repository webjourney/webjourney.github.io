Franklin Breaks Record

Franklin broke a 32-year-old heat record Sunday. The high temperature reached 87 degrees, breaking the old record of 82 degrees set in 1986. It was warm throughout the county Sunday, reporting a high of 82 degrees.The warm weather is due to a strong ridge of high pressure over the county, which is also causing gusty high pressure winds. About midnight Sunday, the primary meteorological tower recorded wind gusts up to 60 mph. High pressure winds are breaking records. Franklin City reached a preliminary temp of 88° today breaking the record of 82° set back in 1986. Gusts of up 50 mph are predicted through Monday morning for parts of the county, including Highway 65. The county could see more record-breaking temperatures Monday.The wind pattern will keep the area dry for at least the next 10 days.
